A lot of open-source projects don't fail over time because the idea was bad. They fail because maintaining them is a lonely journey, and finding a successor is incredibly difficult.

We saw this recently when the ngneat organization vanished from GitHub, leaving critical tools like [ngneat/spectator](https://github.com/ngneat/spectator) and other widely used ngneat libraries missing from the Angular ecosystem. When such projects disappear overnight, the whole community feels the impact.

That's why a group of Angular community members and I have teamed up to create **OpenNG Foundation**, a global organization to help the Angular OSS ecosystem.

## What we are building

OpenNG is a community hub, not a takeover machine. The goal is not to fork every library or act as if we know better. We help where it is needed most: on the maintenance effort.

Our main focus is to revive abandoned projects and find people to maintain them. Acting as a community organization rather than a single-person effort should make it easier to attract sponsors and distribute resources where they are needed most.

## What comes first

Our immediate priorities are:

1. **This website**, sharing our mission and how we work
2. **GitHub Discussions**, a place for the community to coordinate
3. **The first cohort of ngneat libraries**, starting with [Spectator](https://github.com/ngneat/spectator) and expanding to the full set announced in our [first cohort post](/blog/first-cohort-selected-repositories)

After that cohort is on stable footing, we will keep evaluating other abandoned projects. Then we plan to help the wider ecosystem: promoting quality projects and connecting them with contributors and maintainers. That broader promotion work requires clear open-source standards first, something we are committed to writing.

## Looking ahead

Down the road, we hope to find sponsors to help with governance costs and to create bounties that support maintenance work. As a community hub, we aim to put that support where it matters most.

The GitHub organization is still early, but the direction is clear. If you care about sustainable Angular open source, [get involved](/community/get-involved).
